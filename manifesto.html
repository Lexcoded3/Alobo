Manifesto

Our Vision for Soroti City East

Hon. Joan Acom Alobo stands for a constituency where every family has access to quality healthcare, every child receives a good education, and every young person can find meaningful work. This manifesto outlines the concrete steps we will take to build that future — together.

Our approach is rooted in listening to the people: market vendors, farmers, teachers, health workers, and parents across Soroti City East. These priorities reflect your voices and your aspirations for a better tomorrow.

We commit to transparency, accountability, and servant leadership — putting the needs of Soroti City East above all else. Every pledge in this manifesto is a promise we intend to keep.

A People-Centered Agenda

This manifesto was developed through extensive consultation with community leaders, women's groups, youth associations, and elders across all wards in the constituency. It reflects the real priorities of real people.

From Pamba to Gweri, from Pioneer to Senior Quarters, the message is clear: our people want action, not just words. They want leaders who deliver visible change in their communities.

Building on Our Foundation

Hon. Joan Alobo has already made strides in health advocacy, education support, and community development through her parliamentary work. This manifesto builds on that track record with bold new commitments.

As Vice Chairperson of the Teso Parliamentary Group, Joan has demonstrated the ability to work across party lines to deliver results for her people. This same collaborative spirit will drive every initiative in this plan.

A Promise of Service

We invite every resident of Soroti City East to hold us accountable to these commitments. This is not just a campaign document — it is a social contract between a leader and her people.

Together, we will transform Soroti City East into a model constituency — where opportunity is abundant, services are accessible, and every citizen can live with dignity and hope for the future.

A Strong Economic Plan for Our People

Soroti City East has enormous economic potential — from agriculture and trade to small-scale manufacturing. Our plan unlocks this potential by investing in market infrastructure, supporting small businesses, and creating an enabling environment for growth.

We will establish a Constituency Development Fund that channels resources directly to ward-level projects identified and prioritised by the communities themselves.

Key commitment: Launch a UGX 500 million revolving fund to provide low-interest capital to women and youth entrepreneurs across all wards in Soroti City East within the first term.

Market Infrastructure & Trade

Our markets are the heartbeat of our local economy. We will upgrade Soroti Central Market with better stalls, sanitation facilities, lighting, and security — creating a dignified trading environment for hundreds of vendors.

We will also establish satellite markets in underserved wards to bring trading opportunities closer to communities and reduce the burden of long-distance travel for small-scale farmers and traders.

Agricultural Value Addition

Agriculture remains the backbone of our local economy. We will invest in value addition facilities — including grain milling, cassava processing, and fruit drying — to help farmers earn more from their produce.

Through partnerships with agricultural extension services, we will train farmers in modern techniques, provide access to quality seeds and inputs, and link cooperatives to regional and national markets.

Digital Financial Inclusion

We will promote digital banking and mobile money services across the constituency, ensuring that every resident — regardless of location — can access financial services, receive payments, and save securely.

Working with financial institutions, we will facilitate financial literacy training in every ward, helping families build savings, access credit responsibly, and plan for their economic futures.

Environmental Protection & Climate Resilience

Climate change is already affecting our communities — with unpredictable rainfall, flooding, and soil degradation threatening livelihoods across Soroti City East. Our environmental plan addresses these challenges head-on.

We will lead the largest tree-planting initiative in the constituency's history, targeting one million trees planted across schools, wetlands, and degraded areas over the next five years.

Key commitment: Plant one million trees, restore 500 hectares of degraded wetlands, and establish environmental clubs in every school across Soroti City East.

Wetland Restoration

Our wetlands are vital for water regulation, biodiversity, and flood control — yet they face constant encroachment. We will work with the National Environment Management Authority to demarcate and protect all wetlands in the constituency.

Community-based wetland management committees will be established in each affected ward, with local residents employed as conservation stewards — protecting the environment while earning a livelihood.

Clean Energy & Waste Management

We will promote solar energy adoption across the constituency through subsidised solar home systems and solar-powered water pumps for irrigation. Clean energy means lower costs and healthier homes.

A comprehensive waste management programme will introduce community recycling points, regular waste collection in urban centres, and public awareness campaigns on proper waste disposal and environmental stewardship.

Climate-Smart Agriculture

Farmers will receive training in climate-smart techniques including agroforestry, water harvesting, drought-resistant crops, and soil conservation practices that protect both livelihoods and the land.

We will establish demonstration farms in each sub-county where farmers can learn hands-on about sustainable practices that increase yields while protecting our natural resources for future generations.

Jobs for All — A Youth Employment Revolution

Youth unemployment is the greatest challenge facing our constituency. Too many well-educated young people are idle, their talents wasted. Our Jobs for All programme will create thousands of opportunities across Soroti City East.

We will launch a constituency-wide skills census to identify the talents, qualifications, and aspirations of every young person — then connect them with training, mentorship, and job opportunities.

Key commitment: Create 2,000 new jobs and training placements through public works, apprenticeships, and enterprise support within the first two years of our mandate.

Vocational Skills Centres

We will establish three new vocational training centres across the constituency offering certificates in tailoring, welding, carpentry, ICT, hairdressing, and electrical installation — skills that lead directly to employment.

Each centre will be equipped with modern tools and staffed by qualified instructors. Graduates will receive start-up toolkits and be linked to microfinance institutions for business capital.

Public Works Programme

A constituency public works programme will employ young people in road maintenance, drainage cleaning, tree planting, school renovation, and other community infrastructure projects — providing income while improving our shared environment.

This programme will target at least 500 young people annually, prioritising those from the most vulnerable households. Participants will also receive financial literacy and entrepreneurship training.

Innovation & Digital Economy

We will establish an innovation hub in Soroti City equipped with computers, internet, and co-working space where young people can develop digital skills, work on tech projects, and access online job opportunities.

Partnerships with technology companies and training providers will bring coding bootcamps, digital marketing courses, and freelancing workshops to our youth — opening doors to the global digital economy.

Fair Taxation for Working Families

Small business owners, market vendors, and working families in Soroti City East bear a disproportionate tax burden. We will advocate for a fairer tax system that supports, rather than punishes, hardworking Ugandans.

In Parliament, Hon. Joan Alobo will champion legislation to increase the tax-free income threshold, reduce informal sector taxes, and introduce targeted relief for low-income earners.

Key commitment: Push for raising the tax-free threshold from UGX 235,000 to UGX 500,000 per month, putting more money directly in the pockets of working families.

Simplifying Business Taxes

The maze of local taxes and trading licenses discourages entrepreneurship and drives businesses underground. We will advocate for a simplified, transparent single business permit that replaces the current patchwork of fees.

Small traders and market vendors earning below a reasonable threshold should be exempt from daily market dues — allowing them to reinvest earnings into growing their businesses and supporting their families.

Taxpayer Education & Support

Many taxpayers simply don't understand their obligations or rights. We will launch constituency-level taxpayer education clinics, working with URA to help citizens understand the tax system and access available relief.

We will also establish a taxpayer advocacy desk in the constituency office to assist residents with tax disputes, filing questions, and accessing government services linked to tax compliance.

Transparent Use of Tax Revenue

Citizens are more willing to pay taxes when they can see the results. We will publish an annual Constituency Accountability Report showing exactly how public funds have been utilised and what development projects have been delivered.

Regular town hall meetings will give residents the opportunity to question spending decisions, propose priorities for upcoming budgets, and hold their leaders accountable for every shilling collected.

The Best Schools & Hospitals for Our People

Every family deserves access to quality education and healthcare — regardless of income or location. Our plan invests in both sectors to ensure no one in Soroti City East is left behind.

We will work with the Ministries of Education and Health, development partners, and community organisations to upgrade facilities, recruit qualified staff, and ensure essential supplies reach every school and health centre.

Key commitment: Renovate 10 primary schools and 5 health centres, provide bursaries to 500 vulnerable students annually, and ensure every health centre III has essential medicines in stock.

Quality Education for Every Child

Too many children in our constituency attend overcrowded schools with inadequate facilities. We will prioritise classroom construction, teacher housing, and provision of textbooks and learning materials.

A special emphasis will be placed on girl child education — providing sanitary pads, establishing safe reporting mechanisms for abuse, and creating mentorship programmes that keep girls in school through completion.

Accessible Healthcare

Our health centres must have the medicines, equipment, and staff to serve our communities. We will advocate for increased health sector funding and partner with organisations to stock essential medicines consistently.

Community health outreach programmes will bring services directly to villages — including immunisation, antenatal care, HIV testing and counselling, malaria prevention, and health education.

Maternal & Child Health

No woman should die giving life. We will equip all health centres with maternal health supplies, train more midwives, and establish an emergency transport fund for obstetric emergencies in remote areas.

Nutrition programmes for children under five, including growth monitoring and supplementary feeding for malnourished children, will be expanded to reach every sub-county in Soroti City East.

Securing Our Homes & Communities

Safety and security are fundamental to a thriving community. Every resident of Soroti City East deserves to feel safe in their home, on the streets, and in their place of work — day and night.

We will strengthen community policing partnerships, invest in street lighting, and support neighbourhood watch programmes to create a safer environment for all residents.

Key commitment: Install solar street lights in 20 high-risk areas, establish 50 community policing committees, and create a rapid response fund for emergency security situations.

Community Policing

The relationship between police and communities must be built on trust and cooperation. We will facilitate regular community-police dialogue meetings where residents can voice concerns and collaborate on solutions.

We will advocate for increased police presence in underserved areas and ensure that police posts are adequately resourced with vehicles, communication equipment, and personnel.

Domestic Violence Prevention

Too many women and children suffer in silence. We will establish a constituency-level domestic violence response system with trained counsellors, safe reporting mechanisms, and referral pathways to legal and medical services.

Public awareness campaigns will challenge harmful norms and encourage community members to speak out against violence. We will also support survivors with skills training and economic empowerment programmes.

Youth & Crime Prevention

Idle youth are vulnerable to criminal exploitation. Our comprehensive youth employment and skills programme directly addresses the root causes of crime by providing alternatives — jobs, training, and hope.

Sports and recreation programmes will be expanded across all wards, giving young people positive outlets for their energy and building the teamwork and discipline that serve them throughout life.

Dignity in Retirement — Honouring Our Elders

Our elders built the communities we enjoy today. They deserve to age with dignity, security, and access to the services they need. We will champion programmes that protect and support senior citizens across Soroti City East.

The Social Assistance Grants for Empowerment (SAGE) programme must reach every eligible elder. We will facilitate registration drives to ensure no qualifying senior misses out on this vital support.

Key commitment: Ensure 100% SAGE coverage for eligible elders, establish senior citizens' support desks in all health centres, and create social spaces where elders can gather and stay active.

Elder-Friendly Healthcare

Chronic conditions like hypertension, diabetes, and arthritis disproportionately affect older people. We will establish dedicated elder health clinics at major health centres, with shorter waiting times and age-appropriate care.

Mobile health teams will visit remote villages to provide check-ups, medication refills, and health education to seniors who cannot easily travel to health facilities.

Pension Advocacy & Financial Security

Many retirees wait years for their pensions to be processed. In Parliament, we will push for reforms that streamline pension payments, ensure timely disbursement, and protect pension funds from mismanagement.

We will also promote community-based savings groups for seniors, providing financial literacy training and connecting elderly entrepreneurs to appropriate market opportunities.

Intergenerational Programmes

Our elders possess a wealth of knowledge, skills, and cultural heritage. We will create mentorship programmes pairing seniors with young people — preserving traditional knowledge while building bridges between generations.

Community cultural events will celebrate our elders' contributions, with regular senior citizens' days featuring health screenings, entertainment, and recognition of outstanding community service.

Keeping Our Country & Communities Secure

National security begins with community security. A stable, peaceful Uganda requires well-resourced security forces, effective border management, and communities that are resilient against threats both internal and external.

As your Member of Parliament, Hon. Joan Alobo will support adequate funding for our security agencies while ensuring that security operations respect human rights and maintain public trust.

Key commitment: Support recruitment of local youth into disciplined forces, strengthen community intelligence networks, and ensure emergency response capacity in all wards.

Disaster Preparedness

Floods, droughts, and disease outbreaks threaten our communities with increasing frequency. We will develop a constituency disaster preparedness plan with early warning systems, emergency supplies, and trained community responders.

Working with the Office of the Prime Minister and humanitarian partners, we will ensure that relief reaches affected communities quickly and that recovery programmes help families rebuild their lives.

Food Security

A hungry population is an insecure population. Our agricultural development plan directly addresses food security by increasing local production, reducing post-harvest losses, and ensuring stable food supplies year-round.

We will establish community grain reserves in each sub-county, managed by cooperative societies, to buffer against price fluctuations and ensure families have access to affordable staple foods even during lean seasons.

Countering Violent Extremism

Youth without hope are vulnerable to radicalisation. Our comprehensive youth employment, education, and engagement programme is the most effective counter-extremism strategy — giving young people a stake in a peaceful, prosperous future.

We will work with religious and community leaders to promote messages of peace, tolerance, and unity, ensuring that our diverse communities remain strong, cohesive, and resilient against divisive ideologies.

Conclusion — Our Pledge to You

This manifesto represents more than promises — it is a solemn pledge to the people of Soroti City East. Every commitment, every target, and every initiative outlined here will be pursued with determination and integrity.

Hon. Joan Acom Alobo has demonstrated through her service as Vice Chairperson of the Teso Parliamentary Group and her advocacy in Parliament that she delivers results — not just rhetoric.

We ask for your support, your prayers, and your partnership as we work together to build a Soroti City East where every citizen can thrive. The journey ahead requires all of us — and together, we shall succeed.

How You Can Help

Change doesn't happen from Parliament alone — it happens when communities mobilise. Volunteer with our office, share our message with your neighbours, and most importantly, raise your voice for the Soroti City East we all deserve.

Every conversation and every voice raised brings us closer to the Soroti City East we all envision — prosperous, healthy, educated, and secure.

Accountability & Reporting

We commit to publishing an annual progress report detailing achievements against every commitment in this manifesto — so you can track our performance and hold us accountable to our promises.

Regular town hall meetings across all wards will provide direct opportunities for constituents to question, critique, and guide their representative — because democracy doesn't end at the ballot — it lives in every community engagement.

Together, We Build the Future

Soroti City East deserves a leader who listens, who works, and who delivers. Hon. Joan Acom Alobo is that leader. With your support, we will transform our constituency — for our children, for our elders, and for every family that calls this place home.

Forward together. For Soroti City East. For Uganda. 🇺🇬
